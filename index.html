About me

My name is Sandra Bardot and I'm a post-doctoral fellowship at the HCI-LAB based in Winnipeg, Canada. My interests are mainly focused on resolving some of the key issues with displaying and interacting with information on very small displays, such as smartwatches. In particular, this significant challenge of understanding how best to design interfaces and data visualizations for such a fluid form-factor. I am working on concerns related to interacting with data on small screens in mobility contexts, such as while walking or running for example. Given the limited display space, I am proposing novel visualization techniques as well as new interaction metaphors to manipulate these data visualizations. As part of my role, I am also supervising students and writing grants and collaborate with academic and industrial partners.

I hold a Ph.D. degree in Computer Sciences specialized in Human Computer Interaction from the University of Toulouse, France (Elipse team). I was supervised by Christophe Jouffrais and Marcos Serrano. I focused on design and evaluation of interaction techniques that assist visually impaired people to explore virtual maps using wearables. These techniques provided users with information from a map relying on located audio and vibratory feedback.
